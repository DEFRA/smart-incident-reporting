The Environment Agency is the data controller for the report an environmental problem service on GOV.UK.

A data controller determines how and why personal data (personal information) is processed.

Our personal information charter explains:

  • what we do with your personal information (personal data) in general
  • your rights
  • how to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office

Why we collect personal data

We collect and process personal data as necessary to exercise our official authority set out in law under:

  • the Environment Act 1990
  • Environmental Permitting (England and Wales) Regulations 2016
  • the Environment Act 1995

The lawful basis we rely on to process your personal data is article 6(1)(e) of the UK General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

What personal data we collect

We collect the following personal data from you directly:

  • your name
  • your phone number
  • your email address, if you include it
  • your home address, if you include it

We collect some personal data by placing small files called cookies onto your computer to collect information about how you use our service.

The personal data collected are:

  • your Internet Protocol (IP) address
  • details of which version of which web browser you use

What we do with your personal data

We use your personal data to:

  • respond to you about your report if necessary
  • improve the service by monitoring how it's used and analysing feedback

We may share your personal data with emergency services and environmental response teams outside the Environment Agency if it is essential to deal with the problem you report.

We will not share your personal data with anyone else without your consent, unless we must do so by law.

We do not use your data to make any automated decisions or for automated profiling.

You can remove consent for us to use your personal data at any time by contacting:

Email: enquiries@environment-agency.gov.uk
Telephone: 03708 506 506

Where your personal information is stored and processed

We store and process your personal data on secure servers in the European Economic Area (EEA).

Your data will not be transferred outside the European Economic Area.

How long we keep your personal data

We keep your personal data while an environmental incident is ongoing and for seven years after the incident is deemed concluded, in line with our retention policy.

Changes to this notice

We may change this privacy notice. In that case, the 'last updated' date at the bottom of this page will also change. Any changes to this privacy notice will immediately apply to you and your data.

If these changes affect how your personal data is processed, we'll take reasonable steps to let you know.

This notice was last updated on 26 September 2023.
